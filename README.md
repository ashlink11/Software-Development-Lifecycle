Question: How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

Answer: User needs are often thought of as business needs and Agile formalizes a process for turning user needs into feature tracking and this is called User Stories. The format is "As a... I want... So that..." so that we can make sure the user gets all of their needs met and that we are constantly reminded of their needs, and that a feature hasn't been abstracted away from the need and then changed.

Question: How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

Approaching development, you first want to be a good employee and that means understanding the management style of your manager. This may be Waterfall or may be Agile. If it's Waterfall, there are distinct and ordered stages of the software development process so you would follow that. If your manager practices Agile methodology, then you would start by participating in perhaps Scrum Events such as a Sprint Planning meeting where the Product Owner shares the Product Backlog filled with the User Stories. I hope to incorporate Scrum Events into my personal career and plan personal projects for my portfolio with Scrum-Agile because I know it's time-tested to keep me well organized.

Question: What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

In software development, to be a good team member, it's good to be reliable and transparent. But the structure of the company heavily depends on helping you to be reliable and/or transparent. Being reliable means that User Stories have been developed in a logical way and that you have the literacies to accomplish the task on time. These literacies include the technological literacy, literacy on how to research technology, and literacy on the velocity of projects. Being transparent is much easier if there is a Scrum Board so that everyone's plan can be up for review at any moment and anyone can review the management methodology and ask questions.
